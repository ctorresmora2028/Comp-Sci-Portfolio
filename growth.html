GROWTH

My most recent project, making the website for my chocolate chip cookie recipe, represented an area of growth for me because I didn’t think I’d ever be able to create a website, and surprisingly, I did, and it wasn’t as bad or hard as I thought it would be to create a website. The hardest areas for me throughout these projects were probably trying to figure out ways to incorporate all the requirements on the rubrics and find ways I could add them into the designs I wanted to create. The obstacles I faced were the bugs at times, sometimes I’d get really stuck on a bug and didn’t know how to fix it, but I’d ask for help or just fidget around with things until my code was running smoothly. My level of effort on this project and in this class directly influenced my success because I always made sure to do the assignments assigned and finish them on time, so I’d know how to succeed on projects and understand how to code the right way. Knowing what I know now, I wish I had designed my website a little better if I had more time. I’d try to add in a few harder coding examples from the W3 Schools website.
